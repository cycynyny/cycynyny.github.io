MECH338 Heat Transfer

Course Description

This course explores steady-state and transient (unsteady) heat conduction in one and two dimensions. It introduces numerical methods such as finite-difference and finite-element analysis. Additionally, the course covers free and forced convection, along with radiation heat transfer.

Course Objectives

  • Apply heat conduction theory to steady and unsteady systems.
  • Use analytical and numerical methods to solve heat transfer problems.
  • Understand and calculate convection and radiation heat transfer rates.
  • Use MATLAB or similar tools to analyze real-world thermal systems.

Recommended Textbooks

  • Fundamentals of Heat and Mass Transfer by Incropera, DeWitt, Bergman, and Lavine.
  • Heat Transfer by J.P. Holman.

Interactive Simulations

These simulations are designed to build physical intuition for concepts covered in MECH 338. Each one lets you manipulate parameters in real time and observe the governing equations in action.

Simulation 1: 1D Transient Heat Diffusion

Visualizes the 1D transient heat conduction equation: $$\frac{\partial T}{\partial t} = \alpha \frac{\partial^2 T}{\partial x^2}$$ The top panel shows the temperature profile evolving over time. The bottom panel shows the second derivative ∂²T/∂x² at each point — the quantity that drives the temperature change. Orange bars indicate points that are warming; blue bars indicate points that are cooling. The two diamond markers on the temperature plot show exactly where the second derivative changes sign, i.e., the boundary between warming and cooling regions.

Use the controls to select different initial conditions and thermal diffusivity values. Observe how a higher α causes the profile to flatten faster at the same physical time.
